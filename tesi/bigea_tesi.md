In spring, it moves coastward to reproduce. Cuttlefish is semelparous (although many alternatives theory are still to be confirmed/denied), so the adults die soon after the spawning event. The eggs are attached to the substratum and the hatchlings are already benthic, limiting the larval dispersal potential. Juveniles use coastal areas as nursery and undergo a period of rapid growth before beginning their autumn migration offshore to the deeper parts of the Adriatic Sea. Basing on the bathymetric and oceanographic characteristics of the basin, it is reasonable to hypothesize a certain degree of isolation for the offshore wintering areas.
